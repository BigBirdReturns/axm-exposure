What a green check means — and what it doesn't

  

    
It means. Under the stated compartment model and the fitted
      parameter box, the concentration the model predicts cannot cross the declared threshold —
      proved by the Lean kernel against Mathlib, reproducibly, in CI.

    
It does not mean the drug or dose is safe for a patient. The proof is about
      the model, not the patient. Model misspecification, CI miscalibration, and off-model
      physiology are out of scope.

  

  
Verify it yourself — nothing here asks for trust:
    each report carries the sha256 of the exact emitted artifact, and
    cd lean && lake exe cache get && lake build re-checks every proof locally.


  
The certified subset

  

    
One-compartment infusion. Steady-state exposure ceiling
      R/(ke·V) ≤ T for all t ≥ 0.

    
Repeated IV bolus. Steady-state therapeutic window
      C_trough ≥ C_eff ∧ C_max ≤ C_tox (rational envelope, auto-emittable).

    
Two-compartment infusion. Steady-state central exposure
      R/(ke·V1) (peripheral cancels) — plus a machine-checked all-time no-overshoot bound.

    
Everything else → fail closed. ≥3 compartments, nonlinear elimination,
      unsupported routes: a precise reason, no proof emitted.

  


  
How the verdicts work

  
certified (exit 0)  in subset, certificate holds   → emits kernel-checkable Lean
failed    (exit 2)  in subset, condition violated  → a real finding, no Lean
refused   (exit 3)  outside the certified subset    → won't certify what it can't prove
